Incident — Initial Injury
Self-reported by patient
Aug 14, 2024

Patient Maria Martinez, age 42, presented to ER following alleged misadministration of medication during routine outpatient procedure at Allied Health Systems facility. Patient reports immediate onset of severe abdominal pain, nausea, and dizziness within 30 minutes of IV medication administration.

Incident Key Finding
Emergency Department Visit
St. Mary's Hospital — Dr. James Liu, M.D.
Aug 14, 2024

Patient arrived via ambulance at 14:32. Chief complaint: severe abdominal pain (8/10), nausea, tachycardia. Initial vitals: BP 148/92, HR 112, Temp 99.1F. IV fluids administered. Abdominal CT ordered.

Admitted for observation. Pain management initiated with morphine 4mg IV.

Treatment Key Finding

CT Abdomen & Pelvis with Contrast
St. Mary's Hospital — Radiology Dept.
Aug 14, 2024

Findings: Moderate inflammation of gastric lining consistent with chemical irritation. No perforation. Small amount of free fluid in peritoneal cavity. No masses or obstructive pathology identified.

Diagnosis — Chemical Gastritis, Medication Error
St. Mary's Hospital — Dr. James Liu, M.D.
Aug 16, 2024

Final diagnosis: Acute chemical gastritis secondary to medication administration error. Patient records from Allied Health confirm wrong medication dosage — 10x prescribed concentration of potassium chloride administered intravenously. Discharge after 48-hour observation with follow-up instructions.

Diagnosis Key Finding

Follow-up Visit

Patient reports persistent epigastric pain (5/10), intermittent nausea, difficulty tolerating solid foods. Weight loss of 8 lbs since incident. Physical exam reveals epigastric tenderness. Prescribed omeprazole 40mg daily. Ordered upper endoscopy.

Findings: Erythematous gastric mucosa with scattered erosions in the antrum and body. Biopsy taken from 3 sites. Consistent with resolving chemical gastritis. H. pylori negative. Recommended continued PPI therapy and bland diet.
